## 1. The "Slow Startup" Crawl
**The Difficulty:** You press the power button, go make a cup of tea, drink it, and your PC is *still* booting up.
**The Fix:** Too many programs are trying to open simultaneously.
*   **Windows:** Press `Ctrl + Shift + Esc` to open Task Manager. Click the "Startup" tab. Right-click and "Disable" non-essential programs like Spotify or Skype from starting automatically.

## 2. The Random Wi-Fi Drop (No Internet Access)
**The Difficulty:** The Wi-Fi icon shows a yellow triangle or disconnects randomly during important Zoom calls.
**The Fix:** 
1.  **Forget the Network:** Go to Wi-Fi settings, select your network, and click "Forget". Reconnect with your password.
2.  **Flush the DNS:** Open Command Prompt as Administrator and type `ipconfig /flushdns` and press Enter. 
3.  **Update Drivers:** In Device Manager, find your Network Adapter, right-click, and select "Update driver".

## 3. "The Blue Screen of Death" (BSOD)
**The Difficulty:** Your screen turns blue with a sad face `:(` and a bunch of white text. Your unsaved work is gone.
**The Fix:** Don't panic. The BSOD is Windows protecting itself from hardware or driver failure.
*   Note down the "Stop Code" at the bottom (e.g., `MEMORY_MANAGEMENT`). 
*   Restart the PC. Usually, it's a one-off error. 
*   If it keeps happening, it's likely failing RAM or a corrupted driver update. This requires professional diagnostics.

## 4. The Laptop Sounds Like a Jet Engine (Overheating)
**The Difficulty:** Your laptop fan is incredibly loud, the bottom burns your lap, and the system slows down.
**The Fix:** Dust is blocking the air vents, preventing the heat from escaping.
*   Elevate your laptop on a hard surface (never on a bed or pillow).
*   Use a can of compressed air to blow out the side and bottom vents.
*   If it persists, the thermal paste inside has dried out. **We offer internal deep cleaning and thermal paste replacement starting at just ₹999.**

## 5. The "My Printer Won't Print" Mystery
**The Difficulty:** The document is sent, the printer is on, but nothing happens.
**The Fix:**
1.  Clear the print queue: Go to Printers & Scanners, click your printer -> "Open queue", and cancel all documents.
2.  Turn off the printer, unplug it from the wall for 30 seconds, and plug it back in.
3.  Check the Wi-Fi connection. Ensure the printer and the PC are on the *exact same* Wi-Fi band (2.4GHz vs 5GHz).
